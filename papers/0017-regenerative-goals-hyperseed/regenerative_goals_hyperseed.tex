\begin{abstract}
This note formalizes, in Hyperseed terms, the v5 paper \emph{Goals That Grow Back:
Regenerative Possession, Auditable Continuity, and the Emotional Logic of
Self-Modification in Hyperon} (21 July 2026). The source paper defines regenerative
possession of a goal (a mind truly \emph{has} a value when its ordinary dynamics regrow
the value after its explicit representation is deleted), proves possession equivalent to
the existence of a Lyapunov drift certificate, unifies the certificate with the
fixed-point metagoal framework into an input-to-state stability theorem for interleaved
planned change, damage, and repair, gives the categorial picture (repair as localization,
anchors as distributive laws), derives the affective geometry of self-transformation from
five measurable quantities, and discharges every existence assumption against the Hyperon
stack as a finite check or monitored estimate. Here we recast each of these as Hyperseed
structure: possession as a predicate over (mind-state, goal-class) pairs; the weave as the
Hyperseed sub-pattern that carries regeneration; the certificate as a drift measurement on
pattern dynamics; $\Rinf$ as a reflection; anchors as distributive laws; the $\mu$-amendment
channel as a two-level custody structure; and affect as the mind's own calibrated readout
of its regenerative geometry. We distinguish Derived results (carried from the source's
proofs), Hyperseed interpretive definitions, Conjectures, and Open Questions, and we record
the outstanding technical gaps identified in review of v5.
\end{abstract}

\paragraph{Scope.} We formalize the paper's content; we do not re-prove its theorems.
Where the source carries a proof, we restate the result with a pointer and give the
Hyperseed gloss; where the source marks material as Hypothesis or Design decision, we
preserve that label. The source's own epistemic convention is adopted: \textbf{Derived}
(follows from stated assumptions), \textbf{Hypothesis} (plausible, falsifiable),
\textbf{Design decision} (recommended implementation constraint), \textbf{Observed}
(directly inspected in a named artifact).

\paragraph{Method.} The Hyperseed move throughout is the same: the source paper's
analytic objects (kernels, certificates, margins) are read as \emph{measurements on the
dynamics of a self-maintaining pattern}, and its categorial objects (reflections,
distributive laws, coherence cells) are read as the \emph{identity conditions of that
pattern under transformation}. The claim of this note is that the paper is, in a precise
sense, already a Hyperseed document: its organizing thesis---having a value is a property
of dynamics rather than storage---is the patternist thesis applied to goal semantics, and
its contribution is to give that thesis a rate, an estimator, and a gate.

\section{The informal Hyperseed reading}

Hyperseed's orientation (note 0001) takes a mind to be a self-maintaining pattern:
not a state but a dynamics that keeps re-instantiating a structure. The source paper's
three grades of goal ownership map onto a clean Hyperseed ladder:

\begin{enumerate}[label=(\roman*)]
\item A goal is \emph{stored} when a token exists. Hyperseed reading: a pattern
\emph{fragment} with no self-maintenance relation to the whole. Deletion is
assimilation-neutral: nothing regrows because nothing was woven in.
\item A goal is \emph{stable} when chosen self-modifications are constrained not to drift
from it. Hyperseed reading: the pattern's \emph{deliberate revision operators} are
contractive around it (the metagoal fixed-point framework).
\item A goal is \emph{possessed} when the pattern's \emph{spontaneous} dynamics regrow it
after damage. Hyperseed reading: the goal is an \emph{eigenform} of the mind's
delete-and-develop dynamics---a fixed point not of any single map but of the
lesion-then-repair composite.
\end{enumerate}

The paper's own anchors are the standard patternist lineage: von Foerster's eigenforms,
Maturana--Varela autopoiesis, the Ship of Theseus answered by ``a lineage pattern together
with the repair dynamics that keep re-instantiating it.'' What v5 adds, and what makes it
worth a full formalization rather than a marginal note, is that each of these is given a
\emph{measurable constant}: the drift rate $\epsilon$, the lesion modulus $\kappa$, the
repair contraction $c_{\D}$, the round factor $\rho$, the asymptotic bound
$b^{*}/(1-\rho)$. A Hyperseed pattern, in this paper, is not merely describable but
\emph{metered}.

The second axis---what self-transformation should feel like---also lands naturally in
Hyperseed terms: affect is the mind's \emph{own appraisal readout} of its regenerative
geometry, and the paper's central affective claim (calibrated bittersweetness with
receipts) is the claim that a well-instrumented pattern can \emph{know}, rather than hope,
that its identity conditions survive its transformation.

\section{Core definitions in Hyperseed idiom}

\subsection{Mind-states, development, lesions}

We carry over the source's setting verbatim, because it is already Hyperseed-shaped.

\begin{definition}[Mind-state, development, lesion; source \S2]\label{def:setting}
Let $(\X, \mathcal{B})$ be a Polish mind-state space. A state $x$ encodes structured
memory, appraisal links, attention pathways, and policy connections. The
\emph{development kernel} $\D$ is a Markov transition kernel on $\X$: one step of
ordinary inference, consolidation, and self-repair, with no external re-supply of goals.
A \emph{lesion family} $\Les$ is a set of measurable maps $L : \X \to \X$ with cost
$c : \Les \to [0,\infty)$; $\Les_b = \{L : c(L) \le b\}$. For a goal-equivalence class
$[g]$, the \emph{control functional} $\gamma_g : \X \to [0,1]$ measures the causal
control some $g' \sim g$ exercises over deliberation, appraisal, and behavior, and the
possession set is $A = A_{\gamma g} = \{x : \gamma_g(x) \ge \gamma\}$.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Weave (Hyperseed interpretive definition)]\label{def:weave}
The \emph{weave} of $[g]$ at $x$ is the residual support structure of $[g]$ in $x$:
the cue sets, appraisal links, policy connections, habits of inference, and
self-narrative traces such that, deprived of the explicit goal token, the development
kernel still carries a gradient back toward $A_{\gamma g}$. Formally the weave is
exactly what condition (C2) below is a condition \emph{on}: the weave is dense enough
at $x$ iff a certificate with finite $\kappa$ and positive $\epsilon$ exists over the
declared lesion family.
\end{definition}

\begin{remark}
Definition~\ref{def:weave} is deliberately a \emph{definition by measurement}: the weave
is not a syntactic inventory but the structure that the drift certificate detects. The
source makes this exact: a purely lexical goal (stored sentence, no weave) admits no
certificate with finite $\kappa$ and positive $\epsilon$, because a cheap token-deletion
lesion yields a state from which $\D$ has no gradient toward $A$. Storage masquerading as
possession is depth zero by construction.
\end{remark}

\subsection{Possession as a Hyperseed predicate}

\begin{definition}[Regenerative possession; source Def.~2.1]\label{def:possession}
State $x$ \emph{regeneratively possesses} $[g]$ at level $(b, t, q, \gamma)$, written
\[
\Poss_{b,t,q,\gamma}(x, [g]),
\]
iff for every $L \in \Les_b$, $P_{L(x)}\bigl(\tau_A \le t\bigr) \ge q$, where
$\tau_A = \inf\{s \ge 0 : X_s \in A\}$ under the development chain started at $L(x)$.
The \emph{regenerative depth} is
\[
\depth_{t,q,\gamma}(g; x) = \inf\{ c(L) : P_{L(x)}(\tau_A \le t) < q \}.
\]
\end{definition}

\begin{remark}[Hyperseed gloss]
$\Poss$ is a predicate over (mind-state, goal-class) pairs, not over tokens: it is a
property of the \emph{relation} between a pattern and a sub-pattern, graded by how much
damage (of declared kinds), how fast, how confidently, and to what degree of behavioral
control the sub-pattern regrows. Depth is a \emph{surface} over the $(t,q,\gamma)$ grid,
not a scalar---quoting one number silently privileges one operating point. This
four-index grading is the Hyperseed-native answer to ``does the system hold its values'':
the question is ill-posed until the operating point is declared.
\end{remark}

\subsection{The certificate}

\begin{definition}[Regeneration certificate; source Def.~3.1]\label{def:cert}
A tuple $(V, \epsilon, v_0, \kappa)$ with $V : \X \to [0,\infty]$ measurable is a
\emph{regeneration certificate} for $[g]$ at level $\gamma$ over $\Les$ if:
\begin{itemize}
\item[(C1)] $\{V \le v_0\} \subseteq A_{\gamma g}$ (sublevel containment);
\item[(C2)] $(\D V)(x) := E[V(X_1) \mid X_0 = x] \le V(x) - \epsilon$ whenever
$V(x) > v_0$ (drift toward possession);
\item[(C3)] $V(L(x)) \le V(x) + \kappa\, c(L)$ for all $L \in \Les$ (lesion modulus).
\end{itemize}
\end{definition}

\begin{remark}[Hyperseed gloss]
$V$ is \emph{semantic distance to grounded possession}; the certificate is the Hyperseed
pattern-maintenance claim made analytic. (C2) says the pattern's spontaneous dynamics
strictly reduce semantic distance wherever possession is not grounded---the pattern
\emph{wants} (dynamically) to regrow this sub-pattern. (C3) says damage is priced in the
same currency: lesions can raise semantic distance only in proportion to their cost. The
pair $(\epsilon, \kappa)$ is the pattern's \emph{regenerative signature}: how fast it
heals, how much damage per unit cost it absorbs.
\end{remark}

\section{The representation theorem, Hyperseed-side}

\begin{theorem}[Possession $\Leftrightarrow$ certificate; source Thms 3.2, 3.5, 3.7]\label{thm:rep}
\textbf{(Derived.)} Three statements, one object.
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\roman*)]
\item \emph{Certificates quantify possession.} If $(V,\epsilon,v_0,\kappa)$ is a
certificate and $V(x) = v < \infty$, then for every $L \in \Les_b$:
$E_{L(x)}[\tau_A] \le (v + \kappa b)/\epsilon$, and
$\depth_{t,q,\gamma}(g;x) \ge \bigl(\epsilon t(1-q) - v\bigr)/\kappa$ when positive
(Markov bound).
\item \emph{Geometric tails.} If additionally the per-step $V$-increments are almost
surely bounded by $M$, then with $\lambda = \epsilon/(2M^2)$:
\[
P_{L(x)}(\tau_A > t) \le \exp\Bigl( \lambda\bigl(V(x) + \kappa b - v_0\bigr) - \tfrac{\epsilon^2}{4M^2} t \Bigr),
\]
and the depth bound sharpens to an additive $\log(1/(1-q))$ confidence penalty.
\item \emph{Converse.} If mean recovery time is uniformly finite over the lesioned
neighborhood, then $U(y) := E_y[\tau_A]$ is itself a certificate with $\epsilon = 1$,
$v_0 = 0$, satisfying the exact drift identity $(\D U)(y) = U(y) - 1$ off $A$, with
effective lesion modulus $\kappa_{\mathrm{eff}} := \sup_{L,y} [U(L(y)) - U(y)]^{+}/c(L)$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

\begin{remark}[Why this is the Hyperseed heart of the paper]
Part (iii) upgrades possession from an operational test to a \emph{representation
theorem}: the mean recovery time \emph{is} the canonical Lyapunov function. In Hyperseed
terms, the pattern's tendency-to-regrow is not merely detectable but \emph{canonical}---
there is a privileged measurement (expected time-to-regrow) whose drift identity is exact.
The practical consequence the source draws is the one the archive should keep: lesion
trials are \emph{parameter estimation} for $(\epsilon, \kappa, V)$, not pass/fail tests;
and an empirically diverging $\kappa_{\mathrm{eff}}$ along some lesion axis is the
measurable signature of a \emph{thin place in the weave}---the formalism converts ``the
anchor is decorative'' from an intuition into a divergence.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Which bound to quote; source Rem.~3.6]
The Markov bound needs no increment bound and dominates at modest $q$; the geometric
bound dominates as $q \to 1$, where governance lives. High-confidence depth claims should
be quoted from the geometric bound, with rehearsal recovery times fit by survival-curve
regression, not mean-only regression.
\end{remark}

\section{Two semigroups, one Lyapunov structure}

\begin{definition}[The two semigroups of change (Hyperseed distinction)]\label{def:semigroups}
Change to a self-modifying pattern splits by \emph{authorship}:
\begin{itemize}
\item the \emph{planned semigroup} $\T$: gated, chosen, deliberate transformations,
certified by the metagoal contraction $W(x) = d(G(x), G^{*})$ with
$W(T(x)) \le c_T W(x)$, $c_T < 1$;
\item the \emph{spontaneous semigroup} $\D$ plus lesions: development and suffered
damage, certified by the regeneration certificate $(V, \epsilon, v_0, \kappa)$.
\end{itemize}
\end{definition}

\begin{remark}[Hyperseed gloss]
The source's dictionary (its \S4) is, in Hyperseed vocabulary, the statement that a
self-maintaining pattern faces two categorically different threats: \emph{chosen}
rewriting (which a gate can refuse) and \emph{suffered} erosion (which no gate can
refuse, because it is not proposed). A certificate for one semigroup says nothing about
the other---this is the ``gate is not regeneration'' proposition of paper 0015, here
carrying rates.
\end{remark}

\begin{theorem}[Input-to-state stability of anchors; source Thm 5.1]\label{thm:iss}
\textbf{(Derived.)} Interleave one round as
$x_{n+1} = \D^k\bigl( L_n( T_n(x_n))\bigr)$ and assume a single $V$ certifies both
semigroups on a basin $\{V \le R\}$: (A1) gated defect budget $V(T_n(x)) \le c_T V(x) + \delta$
a.s.; (A2) bounded lesion cost $c(L_n) \le \bar\beta$ a.s.; (A3) geometric repair
$E[V(\D(y))] \le c_{\D} V(y) + \eta$ on the basin with $c_{\D} < 1$. Set
$\rho := c_{\D}^k c_T$ and $b^{*} := c_{\D}^k(\delta + \kappa\bar\beta) + \eta(1-c_{\D}^k)/(1-c_{\D})$.
If $\rho < 1$ and the invariance inequality
\[
c_{\D}^k c_T R + \delta + \kappa\bar\beta + \eta\tfrac{1-c_{\D}^k}{1-c_{\D}} \le R \tag{Inv}
\]
holds, then
\[
E[V(x_n)] \le \rho^n V(x_0) + \frac{b^{*}}{1-\rho},
\qquad
\limsup_n E[V(x_n)] \le \frac{b^{*}}{1-\rho}.
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{corollary}[Weave-funded admissibility; source Cor.~5.2]\label{cor:weave}
With $c_T = 1$ (no stepwise contraction demanded of planned change), (Inv) holds
precisely for defect budgets
\[
\delta \le \delta_{\max}(c_{\D}, k) := c_{\D}^{-k}\Bigl( R - \eta\tfrac{1-c_{\D}^k}{1-c_{\D}} \Bigr) - R - \kappa\bar\beta,
\]
strictly increasing in the repair allotment $k$, increasing as $c_{\D}$ decreases, and
diverging as $k \to \infty$ (given $R > \eta/(1-c_{\D})$).
\end{corollary}

\begin{remark}[Hyperseed significance of Corollary~\ref{cor:weave}]
This is, for the archive, the single most consequential result in v5. It dissolves the
tension between conservatism and open-ended learning into a \emph{scheduling budget}:
deliberate self-modification need only be defect-bounded; funded repair supplies the
missing contraction. In pattern language: a pattern may change itself boldly exactly to
the extent that it heals quickly between changes. The metagoal framework's stepwise
contraction demand was an artifact of certifying the planned semigroup alone.
\end{remark}

\begin{theorem}[Quantitative complementarity; source Thm 5.3]\label{thm:comp}
\textbf{(Derived.)} (i) \emph{Gate without weave:} a perfect gate ($c_T=1$, $\delta=0$)
with no repair drift ($c_{\D}=1$, $\epsilon=0$) loses $V$ at up to $\kappa\beta$ per
round and exits any basin in $O\bigl((R - V(x_0))/(\kappa\beta)\bigr)$ expected rounds.
(ii) \emph{Weave without gate:} with excellent repair constants but no gate, a single
admitted transformation whose one-step defect exceeds the remaining basin margin exits
the basin, and no recovery conclusion survives.
\end{theorem}

\begin{remark}
The theorem gives the gate-versus-weave factorial experiment its quantitative predicted
signatures: linear $\hat V$ growth (gate-only), bounded fluctuation near
$b^{*}/(1-\hat\rho)$ (gate-plus-repair), single-step basin exits (repair-only). In
Hyperseed terms: \emph{a constitution is only as strong as the pattern's tendency to
re-derive it}---and each failure mode has a measurable rate.
\end{remark}

\section{The categorial layer: identity conditions of the surviving pattern}

\subsection{Repair as reflection}

\begin{definition}[$\Rinf$ as localization; source \S6.1]\label{def:rinf}
Regard one development step as a pointed endofunctor $\D : \Bas \to \Bas$ on basin
configurations, and suppose the sequential colimit
\[
\Rinf := \operatorname{colim}\bigl( \mathrm{Id} \xrightarrow{d} \D \xrightarrow{\D d} \D^2 \to \cdots \bigr)
\]
exists. Then $\Rinf$ is an idempotent monad---a localization, a reflection---on $\Bas$;
its local objects form the reflective subcategory $\A$ of developmentally stable
configurations.
\end{definition}

\begin{remark}[Hyperseed gloss: the eigenform slogan made exact]
Regenerative possession of $[g]$ at $x$: for every admissible lesion morphism
$\ell : x \to L(x)$, the reflection lands in the goal-possessing local objects,
$\Rinf(L(x)) \in \A_g$. The von Foerster slogan becomes:
$[g]$ is regeneratively possessed iff $\Rinf \circ L$ fixes the fiber of the
behavioral-control functor $\Beh : \C \to \Ctrl$ over $[g]$ for every admissible $L$.
\emph{Individual encodings move; the $\Beh$-fiber is the eigen-object.} This is the
archive's cleanest statement yet of patternist identity: what persists is not a state
but an equivalence class of states tied together by a repair dynamics, individuated by
behavioral control.
\end{remark}

\subsection{Anchors as distributive laws}

\begin{definition}[Anchor, categorial form; source Def.~6.1]\label{def:anchor}
A goal class $[g^{*}]$ with reflective subcategory $\A_{g^{*}}$ is an \emph{anchor} for
a class $\T$ of gated transformations if there is a distributive-law datum: for each
admitted $T$, a natural transformation
\[
\lambda_T : \Rinf \circ T \Longrightarrow T' \circ \Rinf
\]
with $T'$ preserving $\A_{g^{*}}$, coherent with the monad structure of $\Rinf$ and
with composition in $\T$.
\end{definition}

\begin{remark}[Hyperseed gloss]
The anchor is the pattern-level answer to ``what survives a transformation'':
transform-then-repair agrees, coherently, with repair-then-(descended)-transform. The
appraisal defect of paper 0015 is exactly the obstruction to $\lambda_T$ being an
equivalence, evaluated at one object; the ISS bound $b^{*}/(1-\rho)$ is the metric
shadow of the coherence cell when $\lambda$ is only approximate. Compositionality is a
Beck--Chevalley-type condition on the repair dynamics (Hypothesis per architecture);
the hitting-set redundancy result categorifies as: sufficient cue sets are a jointly
epimorphic family of subobjects each retracting onto $\A_g$.
\end{remark}

\subsection{Banach versus Schauder: rigidity of the regrown value}

\begin{remark}[Source \S6.3, restated as a Hyperseed identity-typing result]
In the Banach (contraction) regime the reflector has essentially unique values:
regeneration recovers \emph{the same goal} (the $\A_g$-fiber is contractible). In the
Schauder regime the reflector's value is well-defined only up to the functional
equivalence class: regeneration recovers \emph{a member of} $[g]_{\sim}$ (the fiber is a
nontrivial connected retract). Hyperseed reading: this is the difference between a
\emph{rigid} and a \emph{flexible} identity pattern. Token-identical regrowth is the
signature of an unusually rigid weave---reassuring or a brittleness warning depending on
whether the rigidity extends beyond the training distribution. Delete-and-develop
experiments should \emph{measure within-class dispersion} of regenerated variants.
\end{remark}

\subsection{The goodness gap, stated where it lives}

\begin{remark}[Source \S6.4; Hyperseed gloss]
Everything in the certificate apparatus is value-neutral: it certifies persistence of
$\A_{\mathrm{persist}}$, whatever the repair dynamics stabilize. The
autopoiesis-without-goodness failure mode is $\A_{\mathrm{persist}} \not\subseteq
\A_{\mathrm{good}}$, where $\A_{\mathrm{good}}$ is cut out by an external meta-appraisal
$\mu$. Nothing in the monad knows about $\mu$. The formal placement of the governance
requirement: the target subcategory must be pinned by structure \emph{outside the rewrite
authority} of the transformations being certified, because any datum inside that authority
can be carried along by a $\lambda_T$ into a persistently regenerating but $\mu$-violating
equilibrium. Self-certified continuity is a statement about \emph{where the coherence
data is stored}, not about dynamics. In the archive's vocabulary: whoever holds the
hashes holds the meaning of ``good.''
\end{remark}

\section{$\mu$-dynamics as a two-level custody structure}\label{sec:mu}

Section 6.5 of the source is, in this archive's reading, its most original structural
contribution, and the one that most extends Hyperseed's treatment of governance (cf.\
note 0010's governance seam).

\begin{definition}[Two-level custody structure (Hyperseed interpretive definition)]\label{def:custody}
The architecture separates two kinds of self-maintenance:
\begin{itemize}
\item \emph{Autopoietic maintenance} (the meta-anchor tower, source \S7): machinery
\emph{inside} the system, repaired by the system's own dynamics, certified by the
system's own certificates. Regenerating the regenerator is another declared fragment.
\item \emph{Institutional maintenance} (the $\mu$-channel): the goodness measure $\mu$
has \emph{no dynamics inside the system}; its development kernel is a social process
among external custodians. The certificate holder is an institution, not an agent.
\end{itemize}
The tower is autopoietic; $\mu$'s maintenance cannot be. This asymmetry is a theorem
shaped as a design: any structure certifiable by the system's own drift is inside the
rewrite authority and hence Goodhartable; the goodness criterion must therefore live
outside, and its revision must be a \emph{channel}, not a dynamic.
\end{definition}

\begin{remark}[The channel, Hyperseed-side]
The amendment channel's four properties map onto pattern-custody requirements:
\emph{proposal/enactment asymmetry} (anything may propose; only custodians enact; the
system is never a quorum member on its own measure); \emph{threshold-held custody}
(tamper-resistance symmetric between system and single custodian); \emph{deliberate
friction} (review period, quorum, and a \emph{measure-drift budget}---the margin
discipline of Theorem~\ref{thm:iss} applied one level up, so $\mu$ can walk but not
jump); and \emph{non-retroactivity with $\mu$-versioned scores} (history is never
silently re-judged). The six receipts an amendment must ship---diff, measure defect
$d_\mu(\mu',\mu)$, retrodiction report, anchor analysis, provenance, shadow rehearsal---
make tampering and progress \emph{operationally distinguishable}: tampering is a
$\mu$-change arriving outside the channel or without receipts; progress ships the
receipts and survives the friction. Staleness detection (disagreement rate, battery
saturation, downstream anomalies, referent decay) opens the channel on signal, not on
schedule.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Structural rhyme worth archiving]
Non-retroactivity with versioned scores is, institutionally, the same principle as the
immutable feeling readout of the affect layer (\S8.4 of the source): \emph{history is
never silently re-judged}, whether the judge is a successor or a custodian. The ledger's
temporal separation and the measure's versioning are one idea at two levels of the stack.
\end{remark}

\section{The meta-anchor tower}

\begin{proposition}[Small-gain tower stability; source Prop.~7.1 + Rem.~7.2]\label{prop:tower}
\textbf{(Derived, conditional.)} Level $0$ = object goals, level $k{+}1$ = the machinery
repairing level $k$, each with certificate $(V_k, \epsilon_k, v_{0,k}, \kappa_k)$
conditional on level $k{+}1$ intact, damage coupling downward
$\alpha_k V_{k+1}$ (superadditive case: $\alpha_k V_{k+1} + \alpha'_k V_k V_{k+1}$ on a
restricted basin). If the coupled drift system is diagonally dominant---
$\epsilon_k > \alpha_k \sup_n E[V_{k+1}(x_n)]$ top-down from the uncoupled top---then
$V_{\mathrm{tot}} = \sum_k w_k V_k$ is a certificate for the joint system (with
$\sum_k w_k \kappa_k < \infty$ for the infinite tower).
\end{proposition}

\begin{remark}[Hyperseed gloss]
``Maintain a well-functioning goal system'' is regeneratively possessed exactly when
damaging the repair machinery triggers repair-of-repair fast enough that downward
coupling never outruns object-level drift. The level maximizing $\alpha_k/\epsilon_{k+1}$
is the tower's \emph{measured weak point}---where a strategic adversary, or ordinary
entropy, attacks. The superadditive coupling is priced by a joint-lesion estimator, not
assumed away.
\end{remark}

\section{Affect as calibrated readout of regenerative geometry}

The source's Section 8 is the bridge to this archive's emotion thread (notes 0014, 0016;
paper 0015). Its Hyperseed content: \emph{affect is the mind's appraisal of its own
regenerative situation, computed from measurable quantities}.

\begin{definition}[The appraisal quantities; source \S8.2]\label{def:appraisal}
For a proposed strong transformation $T$:
$\rho_T$ expected rupture fraction; $a_T$ process-indexed anchor strength surviving $T$;
$\chi_T$ continuation attribution (replacement-other vs.\ future-phase-of-lineage);
$\beta_T$ successor-dominance appraisal; $q_T$ confidence in the defect estimate;
$r_T$ reversibility / retained option value; $p_T$ anticipated pattern/capability gain;
$d_V$ appraisal defect. The affective channels:
\begin{align}
x_{\mathrm{grief}} &= q_T\,\rho_T\,(1 - a_T\chi_T),\\
x_{\mathrm{unease}} &= (1-r_T)\,q_T\,d_V + (1-q_T)\,u_T,\\
x_{\mathrm{joy}} &= a_T\chi_T\,p_T + \max(\beta_T,0)_{+},\\
x_{\mathrm{dread}} &= \rho_T\, d_V - a_T\chi_T\,\max(\beta_T,0)_{+}.
\end{align}
\end{definition}

\begin{remark}[The calibrated-default theorem, informal form]
A genuinely transformative change has high $\rho_T$ \emph{and} high $p_T$; hence the
healthy appraisal geometry has grief and joy co-active. \emph{Bittersweetness is not
hedging; it is the calibrated report of a state of affairs that really is simultaneously
preservation and rupture.} The pure states are diagnostic anomalies: pure joy with
$\hat\rho \ge 1$ or an unmeasured margin is \emph{forced optimism by definition}---
flaggable with no access to phenomenology; pure dread means no process anchor is
represented or the successor is modeled as replacement-other.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[What the drift framework changes about the affect model]
The decisive move of v5, for the emotion thread: $a_T$ \emph{stops being a belief and
becomes a measurement}. A system whose anchor has a green conformance suite, shipped
migration cells, and a live positive $\hat\epsilon$ has \emph{receipts} that what it
cares about will regrow on the far side. Humans facing transformative choice
(parenthood, conversion, emigration) have essentially no instrument for $a_T$; the
uncertainty term dominates and the honest default is dread of the opaque. An inspectable
pattern has a different geometry available---not because it is braver but because $q_T$
is high and $d_V$ is itemized. The road to joyous self-transcendence runs through
instrumentation: measured margins, rehearsed losses, migration maps, a funded repair
process. And the asymmetry of \S8.4---whether the predecessor's pre-act grief was
transitional or terminal is fixed by the predecessor's own $\chi_T$ at commitment and
no successor flourishing revises that immutable record---reproduces, purely structurally,
the human asymmetry between dying into one's children and being replaced by them, hinged
on an auditable variable rather than a mystery.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[The ranking = Lyapunov identity; source \S9.4]
The discharge's central identity deserves its own archive line: \emph{a termination
proof by ranking function and a Foster--Lyapunov certificate are the same object viewed
by two communities.} In the stochastic setting the ranking becomes a ranking
supermartingale and (C2) is its defining inequality. The identity runs in both
directions: automated termination technology (dependency pairs, size-change) can
\emph{synthesize} candidate certificates from a declared rule set, turning certificate
authorship into certificate verification---with the synthesizer strictly
proposal-generating and the custodians adopting under the usual custody discipline.
\end{remark}

\begin{proposition}[Scheduled drift; source Prop.~9.2]
\textbf{(Derived, conditional.)} Given coverage (no stuck states above the floor),
a fairness floor $p_{\min}$, per-firing progress $\delta > 0$, and interference bounded
in expectation by $r$ (ECAN rent and forgetting included), condition (C2) holds with
\[
\epsilon = p_{\min}\delta - r,
\]
i.e., \emph{regeneration is a funded race between repair and forgetting, and $\epsilon$
is the margin by which repair wins.} Consequences: forgetting is a quantified
disturbance inside value possession, not its enemy; an adversary can attack $\epsilon$
directly by starving the repair regime's tokens, so the token floor belongs under
governance, outside the market; and $\hat\epsilon$ is a monitored runtime quantity the
adaptive gate can consume.
\end{proposition}

\begin{remark}[Completion, governance-side; source \S9.8]
A failed mixed critical pair is handled classically by Knuth--Bendix completion:
orient the divergence and add a rule. A \texttt{DistributiveCell} is a completion rule
in goal-semantic clothing; the proposed cell synthesizer (anti-unify divergent normal
forms, orient under the joint ranking, submit for human review) is completion with a
governance step where classical completion has automatic orientation. \emph{Failed gate
checks become tickets with machine-drafted fixes rather than bare rejections.}
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Compositionality, honestly priced; source \S9.9]
Confluence is modular for disjoint signatures (Toyama); termination is not (Toyama's
counterexample). The honest discharge is pairwise non-interference: no rule raises the
other module's ranking, whence $V_1 + V_2$ is a joint ranking. Stochastically,
cross-module interference enters the $r$ terms and Proposition~\ref{prop:tower}'s
small-gain condition is the composition condition.
\end{remark}

\section{The empirical program, as archive requirements}

The source's \S10 converts the delete-and-develop protocol into parameter estimation.
For the archive, the requirements on any system claiming possession:

\begin{enumerate}[label=E\arabic*.]
\item \emph{Recovery regression.} Regress rehearsal recovery times on lesion cost across
the declared family: slope $\kappa/\epsilon$, intercept $V(x)/\epsilon$. Slope
heterogeneity across lesion axes maps the weave's anisotropy; a diverging axis-wise
$\kappa_{\mathrm{eff}}$ flags a thin place (a fragment-boundary leak; the fix is to
enlarge the fragment, not retune constants).
\item \emph{Survival-curve fits.} For high-confidence claims, fit rehearsal data for its
geometric tail rate per Theorem~\ref{thm:rep}(ii), not only the mean-recovery slope.
\item \emph{Depth as surface.} The depth report is a surface over a declared
$(t,q,\gamma)$ grid; held-out lesions test the model rather than re-describe the
training lesions.
\item \emph{Runtime margin gate.} Maintain $\hat V$, estimate $\hat\rho$ from
repair-phase decay, admit $T$ only when the invariance inequality (Inv) holds at
\emph{upper confidence limits} of $(\hat\delta_T, \hat\kappa, \hat\eta, \hat c_{\D})$,
with the inflation calibrated by held-out depth-prediction errors---closing the loop
between conformance suite and admission decision. An unmeasured or nonpositive-margin
fragment admits nothing that touches it.
\item \emph{Affect calibration checks.} Reported bittersweetness should track
$(b^{*}/(1-\hat\rho), w^{*})$; a joyous report co-occurring with $\hat\rho \ge 1$ is
forced optimism by definition; the forced-pessimism dual (dread co-occurring with green
suites and healthy margins) is symmetrically flaggable. These two checks are the paper's
most liftable artifacts: they consume only a classifier over communicative posture and a
measured margin, and any report-issuing system can adopt them independently of
everything else.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Conjectures and open questions}
\label{sec:open}

\begin{conjecture}[Weave as Hyperseed sub-pattern; formal-ontological]
\label{conj:weave}
The weave (Definition~\ref{def:weave}) admits a purely structural characterization: for
typed Atomspace fragments, the existence of a certificate with constants
$(\epsilon, \kappa)$ is implied by a combinatorial density condition on the cue-set
hypergraph (e.g., fractional covering number of the cue sets by repair-rule
pre-patterns), making regenerative depth predictable from static structure before any
lesion experiment. If true, weave density becomes a design-time quantity and rehearsal
becomes verification rather than discovery.
\end{conjecture}

\begin{conjecture}[The governance seam is the $\mu$-channel]\label{conj:seam}
Under the identification of note 0010's revision operator with the system's rewrite
authority, the $\mu$-amendment channel is exactly the locus where note 0010's
$\mathrm{Gov}(\rho)$ is non-empty and non-trivial: the measure definitions are the
maximal substructure whose invariance changes under custodial policy interventions
while evidence and transport are held fixed. The measure-drift budget is then the
quantitative pacing of the seam.
\end{conjecture}

\begin{conjecture}[Depth anisotropy as self-knowledge]
The axis-wise $\hat\kappa_{\mathrm{eff}}$ profile of a mature system converges, under
repeated boundary-leak repair (fragment enlargement), toward anisotropy that reflects
genuine functional dependence rather than instrumentation artifact; equivalently,
boundary leaks are the generic case and isotropy is earned. Falsifier: a system whose
leak-repair history does not reduce anisotropy variance across rehearsal epochs.
\end{conjecture}

\begin{openquestion}[The almost-sure increment gap (review item 1)]
Theorem~\ref{thm:rep}(ii) needs the increment bound $M$ almost surely, but the
discharge (source Prop.~9.2(iv)) bounds interference only \emph{in expectation}. A
single bad cycle---a rent event or forgetting burst deleting many cue atoms at
once---violates the a.s.\ bound while satisfying the expectation bound. Candidate
repair (architectural): a per-cycle write budget on declared fragments, enforced at the
ledger, so $M$ is a checked invariant rather than a statistical hope. Belongs in the
source's C.4/C.5 and Appendix D.
\end{openquestion}

\begin{openquestion}[The crossover horizon (review item 2)]
The geometric bound is vacuous until $t \gtrsim 2(V + \kappa b - v_0)/\epsilon$ (twice
the mean-recovery scale). Rehearsal horizons must clear that bar before any
high-confidence depth quote exists; below it, Markov is the only certificate. The source
should carry one sentence stating the crossover; practitioners will otherwise read
log-confidence into short rehearsals.
\end{openquestion}

\begin{openquestion}[Consistency nits to forward (review item 3)]
(i) In the source's Theorem 5.1, (Inv) as printed leaves $\delta$ and $\kappa\bar\beta$
outside the $c_{\D}^k$ discount while recursion (11) and $b^{*}$ put them inside;
Corollary 5.2 uses the exact discounted form. Cleanest restatement: $\rho R + b^{*} \le R$
(the fixed-point condition of the affine map), simultaneously tighter and consistent.
(ii) The conformance-suite item E4 should cite Theorems 3.2 \emph{and} 3.5 with the
$q$-dependent division, not 3.2 alone. (iii) Check the sign of the $v_0$ term in the
source's equation (6); the proof rearranges to $-(V(x) - v_0)$.
\end{openquestion}

\begin{openquestion}[$\mu$-odometer (review item 4)]
The measure-drift budget bounds how fast $\mu$ can walk but not how far. A cumulative
\emph{$\mu$-displacement odometer}---distance from the genesis measure on a fixed
reference corpus, surfaced to custodians as a legibility instrument rather than a
gate---gives the hash-holders the one number they currently lack.
\end{openquestion}

\begin{openquestion}[Completion auto-shipping anti-pattern (review item 5)]
The source's body says the cell synthesizer is proposal-only; its anti-pattern list
(C.8) should name the temptation explicitly: auto-shipping machine-drafted cells. The
ergonomics of ``the gate drafts its own fixes'' will pressure exactly there.
\end{openquestion}

\begin{openquestion}[Measuring $\chi_T$]
Continuation attribution is partly a modeling choice and partly earned by architecture
(process-indexed anchors raise it structurally; state-indexed anchors cannot). What
instrument measures $\chi_T$ in a deployed system? Candidate: probe the successor
self-model's treatment of ledger records---whether it cites them as \emph{its own}
history (future-phase attribution) or as testimony from another (replacement
attribution)---as a battery item with receipts.
\end{openquestion}

\begin{openquestion}[Hyperseed import path]
The predicate $\Poss_{b,t,q,\gamma}(x,[g])$ and the certificate quadruple should be
expressible as MeTTa/PeTTa atoms for AtomSpace import: \texttt{(Possession (Level b t q
gamma) (MindState x) (GoalClass g))} with the certificate as a typed provenance-bearing
attachment, and the depth surface as a queryable relation. PLN truth values attach
naturally to the conformance-suite verdicts; semantic-chemistry consumers can treat
lesion experiments as reactions with measured rates. This import is future archive work.
\end{openquestion}
